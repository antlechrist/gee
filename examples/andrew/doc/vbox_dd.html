In Your VirtualBox, Sorting Out Your VM

Linspired from: http://forums.virtualbox.org/viewtopic.php?f=1&amp;t=364&amp;start=15

I had an existing .vdi containing an operating system of which, due to certain legal restrictions, I was unable to reinstall a fresh copy. So, here’s what I did.

  • Get a live CD distro. I don’t think it really matters, as long as it has dd and support for ‘various’ filesystems. I used the infamous System Rescue CD, second in notoriety only to Shingledecker’s Tiny Core Linux.

  • Mount the iso under CD/DVD-ROM > ISO image file.

  • Create a fresh, dynamically expanding .vdi with more room than you think you’ll need. Mount this image under Hard Disks > Attachments as the Primary Slave.

  • Fire up your fooked .vdi. Hopefully you will have configured things so that you can boot from the CD/DVD drive.

  • Once you’re in, execute

      # fdisk -l

    You should see /dev/sda (fooked) and /dev/sdb (fresh).
